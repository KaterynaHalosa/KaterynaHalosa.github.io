services

Health Check

All our health check appointments include a nose to tail examination generally combined with a booster vaccination. Many pet owners welcome the opportunity to discuss their pet’s overall health or behavioural matters with us.

learn more

Vaccinations

Vaccinations are an essential part of preventative medicine. They protect your pet against viral infections for which there are no specific treatments. Depending upon local risks and your pet’s lifestyle, we will recommend a vaccination programme to suit your pet.

learn more

Nutrition

How do you choose the best food for your pet from the vast range of products on the market? What foods should you choose if your pet has a medical condition? We can help you make the right choice for you and your pet!

learn more

Surgery

Most pets require surgery at some point in their lives. We perform a wide variety of general surgical procedures in our onsite operating theatre. Prior to, and on admission for any procedure, your pet will be fully examined and blood tests may be recommended, especially if your pet is older.

learn more

Fleas and Worms

Regardless of breed or pedigree, all pets can get fleas and worms. We can guide you through the large number of products available and recommend the most efficient and cost effective treatments that suit your situation whilst keeping your family and pets safe.

learn more

Puppies and Kittens

We know just how delightful it is when you begin that lifetime adventure with your new puppy or kitten. Your new pet will be checked over from nose to tail. We’ll ensure that they are properly vaccinated and recommend a parasite prevention programme that suits your pet and family environment.

learn more

Diagnostic Facilities

The surgery is equipped with a range of up-to-date diagnostic equipment including digital radiology, ultrasound, microscopy and electrocardiogram. Blood and urine test facilities are also available onsite. We are able to investigate illnesses quickly and help assess general health before surgical procedures.

learn more

Dentistry

Whilst dental problems are most common in middle aged and older pets, young animals can have them too, for example retained milk teeth or broken teeth. A lot of animals will continue to eat despite advanced periodontal disease, so checking your pet’s teeth is essential.

learn more

About Us

20 years we help your pets!

Magic Paws Veterinary Clinic was founded in 2003 by dr. Patrick Thornton. We offer complex care for small animals, in particular dogs, cats, small rodents, rabbits, ferrets and hedgehogs.

In 2016, reconstruction work on the building had been done, therefore now we have 300m2 of space at our disposal. On the ground floor you can find the waiting room, the red consulting room, X-ray, hospitalisation ward, sterile operation room and maintenance room. On the first floor, there is the second waiting room, the blue consulting room, toilets, laboratory, stomatological room, the staff room and a storage room.
